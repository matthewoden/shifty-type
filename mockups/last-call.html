Last call — the final-word beat

Today the 20th word ends the match on the spot, so the final word is a free, unchallengeable bluff — and you never even see it land. Last call fixes both: the chain filling hands one closing move to the other player — shake on it, or challenge it. The challenge itself is the shipped one: same flag, same confirm sheet, same STANDS / REJECTED stamps.

Dashed green marks the new pieces — everything else is the shipped UI, redrawn.

4

How last call can end

All three roads use rules that already exist — no new mechanics past the beat itself.

  • Shake on it → the chain is complete; highest points wins, ties broken by lives, then longest word.
  • Challenge, STANDS → the caller pays the usual life, and the match completes (the spent life can swing a points tie).
  • Challenge, REJECTED → the fake is struck, its owner pays the life, the chain rewinds to 19 — and the challenger plays on, until someone's 20th word earns the handshake.
