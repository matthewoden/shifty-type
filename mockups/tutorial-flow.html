Full-screen lesson cards between moves. Clearest reading, worst rhythm — every card yanks the player off the board, and the game stops feeling like a game.

A · On-board bubbles ← proposed

The storyboard above. Lloyd talks from inside the board, anchored near the move he's talking about, with the LL-tile avatar. In-world, keeps the chain visible during every lesson, and the two-eyebrow trick (LLOYD vs THE LESSON) lets him lie in character while the teaching stays trustworthy.

Guidance also fades deliberately: beat 3 ghost finish + glowing key → beat 5 fan only → beats 11+ nothing.

Open questions

Tutorial chain length

Mocked at 10 words (~6 scripted + 4 free) so the whole thing lands inside one short session. Full 20 felt long for a lesson; 10 needs a one-off match setting the engine already almost supports.

Which llama

Lloyd (Mellow), introduced as "the Tutorial Llama" — finishing against the easy brain keeps the guaranteed-win promise. Llois and Llarry stay a post-tutorial discovery.

Exit & resume

The shipped ← Home is the exit on every beat — no custom skip chrome. Proposed: leaving early keeps the Home tutorial card alive; only completion retires it. Tutorial state is not resumable across sessions — it's two minutes, restarting is fine.

Pass teaching

Passing (and its life cost) never comes up in the script — deliberately. The shipped Pass? button keeps its own two-step confirm, and the tutorial teaches the happy path. Acceptable gap?
